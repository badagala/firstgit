Frequently Asked Questions

How do you do the analysis?

We screen your genetic data for the presence of “markers” in your DNA - which are single-letter variations - to calculate your lifestyle disease risk, predisposition to
traits, drug response, etc. We have developed a robust algorithm that is used for analysis, based on genetic markers present in your DNA sample. These single-
letter variations are known as Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms or SNPs.

What happens to my sample? What steps do you follow?

There are several steps in the process
. check for buccal swab sample
. DNA extraction and quality checks
. Genotyping (which is the process of reading your DNA code) and data generation
. Data review and quality checks
. Analysis and reports
. Genetic counselling and personalised recommendations/action plans

Do you get better data with blood samples, compared to saliva/swabs?

The accuracy of genotype data obtained from buccal swabs is on par with that of blood samples. Mapmygenome has standardized effective protocols for DNA processing from buccal swabs as well as blood. There are several quality standards in place, for ensuring maximum sample quality and data accuracy. Right from initial plausibility checks to final data review, trained staff adhere to validated procedures in our NABL-certified laboratory.

Does mapmygenome follow data privacy rules?

Yes, absolutely! Every sample registered at Mapmygenome is deidentified with personal information, before being sent to the lab. We use a secure LIMS for lab processing, data analysis and report generation. At all stages in the process, client information and their data are kept secure by restricted access and similar protocols.

If I am marked as high risk, does it imply that I will get that disease?

No, having a high genetic risk does not imply that you will get the disease. For a disease to have a clinical manifestation, there are several other influencing factors such as familial history, lifestyle, clinical history and exposure to environmental factors.
